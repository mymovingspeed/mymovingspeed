What Is My Moving Speed?

Moving speed means how fast you are traveling from one place to another over time. It applies to:

  • • Walking speed
  • • Running speed
  • • Jogging speed
  • • Car speed
  • • Bike speed
  • • Train speed
  • • Bus speed
  • • General travel speed

In simple words, if you've ever asked: What is my current speed? Whats my speed? How fast am I moving? My speed km/h? Then you are trying to measure your moving speed.

How a Moving Speed Test Works

A moving speed test tool usually measures your speed using one of these methods:

1. GPS Speed Tracking

Most modern speed tracking tools use GPS to calculate your location over time and determine speed. Best for: Walking, Running, Driving, Cycling, Train/Bus Travel.

2. Distance + Time Formula

Manual speed calculators use the basic speed formula: Speed = Distance / Time.

Example: Distance = 10 km, Time = 30 minutes (0.5 hour). Speed = 10 / 0.5 = 20 km/h.

This method is used in many distance time speed calculators and speed formula calculators.

Walking Speed Guide

What Is Average Walking Speed?

The average walking speed for most adults is 4 to 5 km/h (2.5 to 3.1 mph).

Factors affecting walking speed:
  • Age
  • Fitness level
  • Terrain
  • Weather
  • Health condition

How to Measure Speed While Walking

Use a GPS speed tracker online or walking speed calculator to answer: How fast do I walk?

Running Speed Guide

What Is Normal Running Speed?

Activity Speed
Jogging: 6–8 km/h
Light Running: 8–10 km/h
Moderate Running: 10–13 km/h
Fast Running: 13–18+ km/h

How Fast Can a Human Run?

Elite sprinters can exceed 35–45 km/h for short bursts. Most recreational runners run 8–12 km/h.

Speed Formula Explained

To calculate speed manually: v = d / t (v=Speed, d=Distance, t=Time).

If you travel 120 km in 2 hours, then Speed = 120 / 2 = 60 km/h.

Popular Speed Conversions

1 km/h = 0.27778 m/s | 1 mph = 1.60934 km/h
